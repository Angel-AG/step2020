About me

My name is Angel Guevara. I am a Computer Science student at Tecnológico de Monterrey (ITESM). I describe myself as someone looking always to learn and acquire new knowledge and skills, and keeping improving through continuous work and by learning from my peers.

I was first introduced to Computer Science when my father bought me an Arduino, I remember the exact date, because it was my birthday. It was a very cool present, I liked the electronics and programming stuff. The projects were interesting and fun to do, and well, my first project was the equivalent to a “Hello, World!” program, but in the electronics world, a blinking LED. I only had it that spring and summer, because I moved to another city to study high school and I couldn’t bring it with me.

My interest for computer science started to grow during high school. I had a basic course of computational thinking and did a school project using Arduino, in which in a team of 4 we built and programed a robot to avoid obstacles in its way. It was in my last year of high school when I decided to focus on software rather than on robotics/electronics, all thanks to a competition called MIT-ZeroRobotics, in which my team and I had to write a program for a SPHERE satellite in order to accomplish a specific task by controlling the satellite's speed, rotation, direction and more through our code written in C.
